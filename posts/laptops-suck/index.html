Laptops suck

December 7, 2020 2-minute read

Modern laptops are unusable. Spending more than a couple of hours on a laptop leads to neck or back pain. At first, it’s just not comfortable, but then it becomes unbearable. The laptop forces you to hunch over it.

Your neck, wrists, and back will eventually be hurt. For me, it was the neck. I just have to come up with something or I couldn’t work.

There’s no such problem with desktop computers: there are monitors on a stand, some people treat themselves with monitor arms. But I didn’t want to bound myself to just one workplace, that was the reason I bought a laptop in the first place.

I like working in coffee places sometimes. I want to stay mobile and I wanted to work from the same computer at home and work.

And I found the solution.

Your display has to be at the eye-level. It solves neck pain. There are plenty of laptop stands, I started with a cardboard one:

It does the job and you can do it yourself in 15 minutes.

The more sophisticated one is Roost Stand from a former aerospace engineer. It could hold up to 100 kg/200 pounds. I upgraded to it after a while.

This solution comes with a price: you can’t use the laptop’s keyboard and touchpad. You have to buy an external keyboard and external mouse/trackpad.

After all, you end up with:

  • laptop stand
  • Bluetooth mouse, can be less weird than mine
  • Bluetooth keyboard

in addition to the laptop and power brick.

You end up with one pound / 500 grams more in your backpack. You are carrying two keyboards and two pointing devices, and you don’t have a choice.

Laptop vendors do not seem to care or even notice this problem. But the problem exists.

I’ve been at the same coworking for a while and within three months three people asked me about the laptop stand and where to get it, and two of them eventually got it.

The laptop form-factor is old. It’s the same screen plus keyboard booklet it was 40 years ago. I think it’s time to come up with something new.
